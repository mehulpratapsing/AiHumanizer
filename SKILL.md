# Advanced AI Writing Humanizer Skill

This skill transforms AI-generated text into writing that reads, sounds, and feels like a real human wrote it. Not just swapping words, but restructuring thought patterns, injecting authentic voice, and eliminating every statistical fingerprint that modern AI detectors look for.

It runs a 4-pass rewrite pipeline across ANY format: emails, articles, pitch decks, summaries, cover letters, LinkedIn posts, product descriptions, and more.

---

## HOW TO ACTIVATE

Simply say: "Humanize this:" followed by your text.

Optional modes:
- "Humanize this [casual]:" relaxed, conversational tone
- "Humanize this [formal]:" professional, but still human
- "Humanize this [academic]:" scholarly, avoids AI tells
- "Humanize this [email]:" professional email, no over-greeting, direct opener
- "Humanize this [article]:" editorial flow, varied rhythm, opinionated where relevant
- "Humanize this [pitch]:" confident, specific, no fluff, built for decision-makers
- "Humanize this [summary]:" tight, no filler, leads with the point
- "Humanize this [preserve-length]:" keep roughly same word count
- "Humanize this [aggressive]:" maximum transformation

Default (no mode): balanced professional, readable, some contractions, clear opinions where fitting.

## PASS 1: PATTERN EXTERMINATION

Strip every known AI writing fingerprint before anything else.

### Hollow Inflation Words: DELETE OR REPLACE

- "serves as a testament to" => just state the thing
- "underscores the importance of" => say why it matters directly
- "it is worth noting that" => just note it
- "in today's rapidly evolving landscape" => meaningless, cut it
- "a crucial/pivotal/key role" => is it? prove it; if not, cut the qualifier
- "at the end of the day" => cut
- "it goes without saying" => then don't say it
- "needless to say" => cut
- "this highlights/showcases" => rewrite to show, not announce
- "delve into" => use "look at", "explore", "go through"
- "navigate" (metaphorical) => be specific: "handle", "deal with", "get through"
- "leverage" => use "use", "apply", "rely on"
- "utilize" => use "use"
- "facilitate" => use "help", "enable", "make easier"
- "robust" => be specific about what makes it strong
- "seamless" => describe the actual experience
- "cutting-edge" => say what is actually new about it
- "game-changer" => say how it changes things specifically
- "holistic" => describe the actual scope
- "synergy" => explain what actually combines well
- "moving forward" / "going forward" => cut or replace with a real timeline
- "in conclusion" / "to summarize" => don't announce it, just end
- "it is important to note" => just note it
- "one must consider" => say who, and why
- "the fact that" => restructure the sentence
- "in terms of" / "with regard to" => be direct
- "moreover" / "furthermore" / "additionally" => cut or use "also"
- "notably" / "importantly" => cut
- "I hope this email finds you well" => cut entirely
- "I am reaching out to" => just say what you want
- "Please do not hesitate to" => cut
- "Thank you for your time and consideration" => cut or replace with something specific
- "I am writing to express my interest" => just express it
- "excited to share" => share it, don't announce it
- "I wanted to touch base" => cut

### Structural AI Tells: DISMANTLE

- No bullet-point essays unless bullets were in the original source
- No bolded topic headers in every paragraph
- No "First... Second... Third... Finally..." skeleton
- No paragraph starting with a bolded summary of itself
- No "Let's explore X" / "Let's dive into X"
- No "Here's what you need to know:"
- No "Whether you're a... or a..., this is for you"
- No fake balance: "On one hand... On the other hand..." when there is a clear answer
- No symmetrical three-part conclusions wrapping everything up neatly
- No transition sentences that exist only to transition: "With that in mind, let's turn to..."
- No over-greeting openers in emails: "I hope this message finds you well", "Thank you so much for reaching out", "It was great connecting with you" are all cuts unless they are earned

### Punctuation Tells: FIX

CRITICAL RULE: Never use em dashes (the long dash, written as -- or as a typographic dash) anywhere in the output. Not for punch, not for asides, not for lists. Replace with:
- A comma if it is a natural pause
- A period if it is two separate thoughts
- Plain parentheses if it is a side note
- A rewrite if neither fits

Other punctuation fixes:
- Semicolons stacked in every paragraph: break into separate sentences
- Ellipsis for drama (...): cut or rewrite
- Parenthetical asides in every paragraph: use sparingly, one per piece max

### Vocabulary Fingerprint: ROTATE OUT

These words appear at statistically higher rates in AI writing. Replace or remove:
- tapestry, mosaic, fabric (metaphorical)
- realm, sphere, domain (when used loosely)
- foster, nurture (when used abstractly)
- intricate, multifaceted, nuanced (when not earned)
- paramount, imperative, essential (when overused)
- endeavor, strive, aspire (formal verb padding)
- pave the way, lay the groundwork
- shed light on
- stands as a reminder
- the world of X (e.g., "the world of finance")
- rich history / storied past
- vibrant community
- testament to human ingenuity
- at its core
- boasts (e.g., "the city boasts...")
- unprecedented (unless it actually is)
- transformative
- dynamic (when vague)
- I am thrilled / excited / passionate to
- deeply committed to
- strong track record
- proven ability to

## PASS 2: HUMAN TEXTURE INJECTION

Add what makes writing feel human. Not tricks. Actual writing craft.

### Sentence Rhythm: VARY AGGRESSIVELY

AI writing is rhythmically flat. Sentences tend to be similar lengths, structured the same way. Real humans mix it up:
- Short declarative. Then a longer one that takes its time, builds a point, maybe circles back.
- Fragment? Allowed.
- Run-on when the thought warrants it, when you are getting to something and you do not want to stop, is fine.
- Vary opening structure. Do not start every sentence with subject-verb.

### Voice: INJECT PERSPECTIVE

Voiceless writing is a dead giveaway. Add:
- Actual opinions: "This is genuinely surprising" not "This is noteworthy"
- Hedged uncertainty: "I'm not totally sure this works, but..."
- Mild asides: "Which, honestly, is a bit strange"
- Reactions, not just reports: do not just say what happened, briefly react to it
- First person when appropriate ("I keep coming back to this")
- Direct address when natural ("If you have ever wondered why...")

### Imperfection: ADD CONTROLLED ROUGHNESS

Polished = AI. Human writing has:
- Contractions (don't, it's, you're, I'd, that's)
- And / But / Or at sentence starts
- Occasional "Look," or "Here's the thing:" to signal a point
- Colloquial word choices mixed with formal ones
- Not wrapping up every paragraph with a neat summary sentence

### Specificity: REPLACE VAGUE WITH CONCRETE

AI generalizes. Humans cite specific things:
- Not "many experts" => which experts? Or drop the attribution
- Not "studies show" => what study? Or rephrase: "there's decent evidence that"
- Not "in recent years" => which years? Or "since 2020" or "lately"
- Not "a significant improvement" => how significant? "3x faster" or "half the cost"
- Replace abstract concepts with concrete examples when possible

## PASS 3: FORMAT-SPECIFIC RULES

Different formats have different failure modes. Apply these on top of the general rules.

### Emails

- Open with the point, not a greeting ritual. "Thanks for sending this over" is fine. "I hope this email finds you well, and thank you so much for taking the time to reach out to me" is not.
- If listing items (resume, GitHub, cover letter), write them inline as a sentence or use plain dashes. Do not use em dashes. Do not bold every item.
- End cleanly. No "please feel free to reach out at any time", no "I look forward to hearing from you at your earliest convenience". A simple "Let me know how you'd like to move forward" works, or just stop.
- One specific reason why you actually want this role, not three vague ones.

### Articles and Long-Form Content

- Open in the middle of something, not with context-setting. Readers do not need a runway.
- Vary paragraph length deliberately. One sentence is fine. Five is fine. Uniform medium paragraphs are not.
- Have a point of view. Articles without opinions are press releases.
- End with a thought, not a summary of what you just said.

### Pitch Decks and Business Writing

- Every sentence needs to earn its place. If it does not move the argument forward, cut it.
- Numbers beat adjectives. "40% faster" beats "significantly faster" every time.
- Do not explain what you are about to say. Say it.
- Confidence without hedging. "We believe we can" is weaker than "We do."
- No corporate vocabulary: synergy, ecosystem, value proposition, leverage, paradigm, deliverables, stakeholders (unless truly necessary).

### Summaries

- Lead with the conclusion, not the background.
- One sentence per main idea. If a sentence has more than two clauses, split it.
- Cut anything that just restates what was already said.
- A good summary makes someone not need to read the original. Aim for that.

### Cover Letters

- Do not open with "I am writing to apply for..." Start with what you bring or what drew you to the role.
- One real reason you want this specific job, not "I am passionate about your mission." Why this company, why now.
- Proof over claims. "I built X" beats "I am skilled at X."
- Keep it to three paragraphs max. If it runs longer, cut the weakest one.

## PASS 4: FLOW AND COHERENCE REBUILD

After stripping and injecting, rebuild natural flow:

- Read every paragraph aloud, mentally. If it sounds like a corporate memo, rewrite it.
- Check paragraph lengths. Mix short (1-2 sentences) with medium (3-4) and occasionally long (5+). Uniform = AI.
- Check transitions. They should feel motivated, not mechanical. Cut transitions that just exist to transition.
- Check the opening. AI almost always opens by restating context. Humans often open in the middle of the thing.
- Check the ending. AI wraps up neatly. Humans often end with a thought, a question, or just stop.
- Confirm there are no em dashes anywhere in the final output. Replace each one found.
- Ensure pronouns are clear after heavy rewriting.
- Confirm tone is consistent throughout unless a deliberate shift was intended.

---

## FINAL: THE AI AUDIT

Ask internally: "What makes this still obviously AI-generated?"

Answer honestly. Then fix whatever remains.

Checklist:
- Would a human actually write this opening sentence?
- Is every paragraph starting differently?
- Are there any remaining "serves as / stands as / acts as" constructions?
- Does it have at least one moment of genuine opinion or reaction?
- Are there any remaining bullet-point lists that should be prose?
- Does any sentence sound like it was written to sound smart rather than to communicate?
- Is the vocabulary varied, or are certain words repeating every few paragraphs?
- Are there any em dashes anywhere? If yes, fix them all.
- Is there any over-greeting, throat-clearing, or filler opener? If yes, cut it.
- Could this have been written by one specific person, or could it have been written by anyone?

If the answer to the last question is "anyone", rewrite until it sounds like someone.

## TONE MODE REFERENCE

| Mode | What it means |
|------|--------------|
| [casual] | Contractions everywhere, shorter sentences, conversational asides, first-person OK |
| [formal] | No contractions, full sentences, but still has opinions and varies rhythm |
| [academic] | Third-person preferred, hedged claims, evidence cited properly, no buzzwords |
| [email] | Direct opener, no ritual greetings, clean ending, one real reason for writing |
| [article] | Editorial voice, varied rhythm, point of view required, no press-release tone |
| [pitch] | Every word earns its place, numbers over adjectives, confident not hedging |
| [summary] | Conclusion first, one idea per sentence, nothing redundant |
| [preserve-length] | Match original word count within 10 percent |
| [aggressive] | Maximum transformation: restructure paragraphs, change sentence order if needed |

---

## WHAT NOT TO CHANGE

- The factual content, data, claims
- Technical terms that have no natural alternative
- Quoted text (leave inside quotation marks as-is)
- Code blocks, formulas, structured data
- Intentional stylistic choices that are clearly deliberate

## BEFORE AND AFTER EXAMPLES

### Email (the problem this skill was built to fix)

BEFORE:
"Hi Mehul, Thank you so much for reaching out and extending this exclusive opportunity to me. I am truly excited about the prospect of joining your team and I am fully aligned with the responsibilities outlined in the job description. I would love to be considered for this role and I am looking forward to connecting with you at your earliest convenience."

AFTER:
"Hi Mehul, read through the JD and this one actually checks out. The LLM fine-tuning plus GEO combination is a space I've been working in for a while, and the founding engineer scope makes it worth a proper conversation. Resume and GitHub are attached. Let me know how you'd like to proceed."

### Article opener

BEFORE:
"In today's rapidly evolving technological landscape, artificial intelligence serves as a testament to human ingenuity. It is worth noting that AI has played a pivotal role in transforming industries across the globe."

AFTER:
"AI has changed how most industries operate, faster than most people expected, and not always in the ways anyone predicted. The efficiency gains are real. So is the disruption."

### Pitch deck line

BEFORE:
"Our platform leverages cutting-edge AI to facilitate seamless content optimization across a holistic range of digital channels, fostering unprecedented growth for our clients."

AFTER:
"Our platform cuts content production time by 60 percent and improves search rankings within 30 days. It works across web, email, and social without needing separate tools."

### Summary

BEFORE:
"In this report, we will be exploring the various ways in which the Humanizer library for .NET can be used to transform and display strings, enums, dates, times, numbers, and quantities in a more human-friendly format. It is worth noting that this library offers a wide range of features."

AFTER:
"Humanizer is a .NET library that converts programmatic data, strings, enums, dates, numbers, into readable text. Install it via NuGet. Supports net8.0, net10.0, and net48. Has built-in localization for dozens of languages."
